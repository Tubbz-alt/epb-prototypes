Renting this property

The Regulations mean that, since 1 April 2018, private landlords may not let domestic properties on new tendancies to new or existing tenants of the Energy Efficiency Certificate (EPC) rating is F or G (unless an exemption applies).

From 1 April 2020 the prohibition on letting F and G properties will extend to all relevant properties, even when there has been no change in tenancy.

Regulations available at https://www.gov.uk/guidance/domestic-private-rented-property-minimum-energy-efficiency-standard-landlord-guidance

Internal or external wall insulation

Solid walls have no gap, so they cannot be fill with cavity wall insulation.

Internal insulation

Internal wall insulation is done by fitting rigid insulation boards to the wall, or by building a stud wall filled in with insulation material such as mineral wool fibre.

External insulation

External wall insulation involves fixing a layer of insulation material to the wall, then covering it with a special type of render (plasterwork) or cladding. The finish can be smooth, textured, painted, tiled, panelled, pebble-dashed, or finished with brick slips. Read more
